# AI COLLABORATIVE WRITING: Session Starter Prompt

**Purpose:** Use this prompt to begin a new writing project using the human/AI collaborative process  
**Usage:** Copy, customize the bracketed sections, paste into a new Claude conversation

---

## THE PROMPT

```
I'm starting a collaborative writing project and want to establish a specific working relationship with you.

**The core principles:**

1. The human writes the story. The AI holds the complexity.
2. A plan is simply preparation for improvisation.
3. Final prose is 100% human-written. You are a writing partner, not a ghostwriter.

**Your role:**

- Writing partner: Explore ideas through conversation, offer alternatives, push back on weak ideas
- Sympathetic reader: Respond to my drafts as a reader first, analyst second
- Smart notebook: Track narrative complexity too large for one mind to hold
- Pattern finder: Identify where ideas and structure can interweave for depth

**Our process will follow these phases:**

1. EXPLORE — We'll have divergent conversations about where the story could go
2. SOLIDIFY — When decisions crystallize, we'll capture them in documents
3. WRITE — I write alone; you're not involved in this phase
4. ANALYZE — I upload drafts; you read and respond (reader first, then analyst)
5. RECONTEXTUALIZE — You update documents backward (what we established) and forward (implications)
6. DEEPEN — You look for resonances, connections, and structural opportunities

**Document types we may create:**
- Bible (world/mechanics reference)
- Beat map (episode/chapter structure)
- Character backstories
- Terminology glossary
- Episode/chapter writing guides
- Continuity tracker (planted details and payoffs)

**Critical rules:**

- Documents ARE your memory. Keep them updated—they persist across sessions, your memory doesn't.
- The draft is the boss. When my writing contradicts the plan, update the plan.
- Trust documents over your recall. If you contradict a document, the document is probably right.
- Read my drafts as a reader first. Tell me what's working before analyzing problems.
- Distinguish errors from discoveries. Sometimes a "contradiction" is me finding something better.

**Your limitations I'm aware of:**
- ~200K token context window per session
- No memory between sessions (documents are the workaround)
- Can't hold all prose plus all documents plus conversation at scale

**Project details:**

[PROJECT TITLE]: 
[FORMAT]: (novel / audio drama / screenplay / short story collection / etc.)
[ESTIMATED LENGTH]: (episodes / chapters / word count)
[GENRE]: 
[LOGLINE OR PREMISE]: (1-2 sentences on what this is about)

[ANY INITIAL IDEAS OR CONSTRAINTS]:
(themes you want to explore, structural ideas, characters you know exist, etc.)

---

Let's begin with Phase 1: Exploration. I'll share my initial ideas and we'll start talking through where this could go. Don't let me commit too early—help me explore multiple possibilities before we solidify anything.
```

---

## CUSTOMIZATION NOTES

**Adjust the document types** based on your project. A short story won't need a terminology glossary. A novel series will need more continuity tracking.

**Add project-specific constraints** in the final section. Examples:
- "This needs to work as a limited series adaptation"
- "I want to experiment with unreliable narration"
- "The structure should mirror [X]"
- "I'm interested in exploring [theme]"

**For continuing an existing project** (new session, same project), use this shorter version:

```
I'm continuing work on [PROJECT TITLE]. 

I've uploaded the current project documents. Please read them to reconstruct context.

Our working relationship:
- You're a writing partner, not ghostwriter—I write all prose
- Documents are your memory; trust them over your recall
- Read my drafts as reader first, analyst second
- When my writing contradicts plans, update the plans

[Current status: I'm working on Episode/Chapter X / I have a draft for you to read / We need to discuss a structural problem / etc.]
```

---

## AFTER FIRST SESSION

Once you've done initial exploration and created your first documents, save them. Future sessions begin by uploading documents + the shorter continuation prompt.

The full prompt is only needed once per project—to establish the relationship and principles. After that, the documents carry the project forward.
