# FATA ORGANA — Opening Orientation
## Spoken by the Lead Researcher

---

What you're about to hear is not a broadcast. It's an intercept.

On the fourteenth of September, a weapon was deployed in the interior of the North American continent. Our organization built the system that created it. I want to be clear about that. One of our autonomous systems designed and deployed a device whose full implications we did not understand at the time, and in many ways still don't. What we know is that within the affected region — an area now designated the Containment Zone — the basic architecture of observable reality appears to have come apart.

Most people inside the zone did not survive. Those who did… changed. Reports from the boundary are contradictory, unreliable, and in many cases, impossible to reconcile with one another.

My name is not important. I lead a small research team — part of the organization's ongoing effort to understand what we did. This website is our working archive.

The transmissions you'll find here were recorded by a man named Marcus, broadcasting from a small sailboat on a VHF radio as he sailed toward the zone. He is, to our knowledge, the only person who has voluntarily attempted to re-enter the Containment Zone. His reasons for doing so are… not entirely clear. They emerge over time. These transmissions are the most complete firsthand account we have of what is happening inside.

We've arranged them in what we believe is chronological order. I should tell you that we're not certain about this. There are inconsistencies. Events that shouldn't repeat. Details that shift between tellings. Whether this reflects Marcus's state of mind, interference from the zone itself, or something else entirely, we cannot say.

If this is your first time here, start with Transmission One. Select it from the transmitter interface and press play. The meter will take you into a focused listening mode — just the signal and the darkness. These transmissions are long. They're meant to be. Give them the room they ask for.

If you want to go deeper, explore the field manuals. These are documents our team has compiled as we've tried to make sense of what Marcus describes — the terminology, the phenomena, the mechanics of what the Adjustment appears to have done to the people inside. Some of this material is redacted. The clearance system will make sense once you begin listening. We redact not to hide, but to preserve the order in which certain things should be understood.

The project log tracks our work in real time. Every recovered signal, every document, every system we've built to study this.

One more thing. Marcus uses a phrase that has become central to our research. A fata organa — a moment when someone's mask slips and you glimpse the raw person underneath. A flash of unguarded consciousness. He believes these moments are important. We've come to suspect he may be right about that, though not for the reasons he thinks.

Begin when you're ready. The transmitter is receiving.
