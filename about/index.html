About

What this is. How it is being made.

The Project

Fata Organa is an audio drama told across twelve transmissions. It is a story about consciousness, loss, and the things we build to avoid facing either one.

The transmissions originate from within a Containment Zone — a place where the rules that hold reality together have come undone. Someone is broadcasting from inside. These recordings are what was recovered.

The Website

This site is not just a home for the podcast. It is a living record of the project's creation. As Fata Organa is written, scored, and produced, this site updates — documenting the process in real time.

The archive contains materials that serve double duty: they are production documents (sound design notes, story bibles, terminology guides) and they are world-building artifacts (field manuals, recovered documents, decoded transmissions). The line between the two is intentionally thin.

When the project is complete, this site will fix in place — a permanent record of both the finished work and the path that led to it.

The Creator

Fata Organa is created by Wiley Davis — a designer, builder, and maker based in Southern California. The project spans writing, sound design, music production, and web development, and is being produced independently.
